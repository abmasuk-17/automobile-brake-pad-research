# Fabrication & Performance Analysis of Eco-Friendly Automobile Brake Pads Using Recycled Materials

## Overview

This repository contains the conference paper and supporting materials for the research presented at the **8th International Conference on Mechanical Engineering and Renewable Energy (ICMERE 2025)**.

The study investigates the fabrication of environmentally friendly automobile brake pads using recycled and natural composite materials as sustainable alternatives to conventional asbestos-based friction materials.

---

## Publication

**Conference:**
8th International Conference on Mechanical Engineering and Renewable Energy (ICMERE 2025)

**Paper Title**

Fabrication & Performance Analysis of Automobile Brake Pad Using Recycled & Eco-Friendly Materials

---

## Research Objectives

- Develop eco-friendly automobile brake pads
- Replace hazardous conventional materials with sustainable alternatives
- Evaluate mechanical and physical properties
- Compare performance with commercial brake pads

---

## Materials Used

- Steel Fiber
- Coconut Shell Powder
- Rice Husk
- Aluminum Powder
- Brass Powder
- Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂)
- Epoxy Resin

---

## Experimental Evaluation

The fabricated brake pads were characterized using:

- Rockwell Hardness Test
- Flexural Strength Test
- Compression Test
- Impact Strength Test
- Static Friction Test
- Density Test
- Porosity Test

---

## Key Findings

- Sample 01 and Sample 02 achieved performance comparable to commercial brake pads.
- Static coefficient of friction reached **0.42**.
- Maximum compressive strength reached **207.25 MPa**.
- Maximum flexural strength reached **39 MPa**.
- Density remained within the commercial range (2.02–2.05 g/cm³).
- Results demonstrate the feasibility of recycled composite materials for sustainable automotive braking applications.

---

## Research Significance

This work demonstrates the potential of recycled and natural composite materials for manufacturing environmentally friendly brake pads while maintaining satisfactory mechanical performance.
